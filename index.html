01 — Orientation

Three letters, three letters, and a question

Before the model directory, the vocabulary. Every entry in this guide is one of these, or a system built from them.

VLM

Vision-Language Model

Understands images and text together — can describe a scene or answer questions about it. No robot body, no actions. Think of it as the "eyes and reading comprehension" that VLAs are often built on top of.

e.g. the backbone inside OpenVLA, PaliGemma inside π0
VLA

Vision-Language-Action Model

A VLM extended to output robot actions — joint angles, gripper commands, end-effector poses — instead of just words. Give it a camera feed and an instruction like "pick up the red block," and it predicts what the robot should do next.

e.g. RT-2, OpenVLA, π0, SmolVLA, Gemini Robotics
WAM

World Action Model

Goes one step further: predicts what the world will look like after an action, not just the action itself. Used to imagine outcomes before acting, or to train on video without needing real robot data for every scenario.

e.g. DreamZero, Cosmos Policy, Genie 3
World Model?

Does it imagine the future?

Explicitly learns or generates future environment states, video frames, or physical-state representations conditioned on actions — not just semantic knowledge picked up during pretraining.

Predictive?

Does it forecast consequences?

Explicitly predicts future observations, states, or consequences beyond the immediate next action — a broader category than "world model."

Planner?

Does it choose between futures?

Evaluates, searches, optimizes, or selects among predicted rollouts. High-level reasoning alone doesn't count — a model must actually weigh alternatives to earn this tag.
